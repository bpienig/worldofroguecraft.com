Deciding what race you want to be:

There are several factors you will want to look at when you are deciding what will be the best race for you. The following is a list of what you should base your opinions on.

1. Appearance
2. Faction (Horde/Alliance)
3. Racial Abilities
4. Starting Stats
5. Being Unique

Appearance - it's all about what race you find most appealing when it comes to looking at them. For me this is the most important decision as no matter what race you choose there isn't such a big difference that you will be gimping yourself. Personally I enjoy the goofiness of being a Gnome and this was all I based my decision on. (Because they didn't have Halflings)

Faction - Do you want to be Horde or Alliance? This decision will instantly cut the selection of what race you will choose by half.

Racial Abilities - This is one of the most important aspects of each race you will want to base your decision on. Despite what you might hear, no class's abilities are worthless. Some could use an improvement to bring them in line with other racial abilities. However, for the races with the strong Rogue abilities for each faction I would recommend you look into Night Elf and Undead. Also, another consideration for PVP is the Gnome's size. Being small in stature makes you harder to see in mass PVP and more able to hide amongst the game's terrain.

Example: When comparing the Escape Artist with Will of the Forsaken, one is instant cast that breaks any of its listed effects and makes the user immune for 20 seconds. With Escape Artist having a 1.5 second cast time and able to be rooted again right away. Normally by the time Escape Artist goes off the effect has worn off. (Being hit extends the cast time and it can be interrupted when using it.) Still, it's not a terrible ability, just could use a slight improvement. Don't forget that just because one race might have much better abilities than another race does not mean Blizzard isn't going to correct the issue in the future as they do have a goal of having all abilities balanced and actively work towards accomplishing it.

When deciding on racial abilities be sure to remember that some abilities are worthless to you as a Rogue. The following is a list of those abilities that shouldn't influence your decision.

Command - Pet melee damage increased by 5%
Axe Specialization - Orcs get +5 to Axe skill
Expansive Mind - Increase Intelligence by 5%

Starting Stats - Once you hit level 60, starting stats really won't make you any less of a Rogue than any other race; the difference will be very minor at best. With that in mind, if you would still like to see what class has what stats I recommend checking out the starting stats for Rogues at wow.allakhazam.com. For Rogues the most important stats are Agility, Stamina, and to a lesser extent, Strength.

Unique - Does being unique matter to you at all? The majority of players are either Night Elves or Undead. If not being like the majority of other players matters to you, then I would suggest one of the other options.

In all seriousness, all races are roughly equal. While some might come out slightly ahead in some ways when compared to another when comparing stats and abilities, it's not enough to where I would sacrifice what I want to be when compared to what I should be to be able to keep up with other races. You'll do fine either way.
